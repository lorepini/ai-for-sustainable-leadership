# rPodcast Study Plan — AI for Sustainable Leadership (C8, 2025-2026)

*Passive learning track for the final exam. Built from the cheat sheet + 17 session decks + mock exam.*

---

## 1. Executive summary

- **Total episodes: 5**
- **Total estimated listening time: ~2 h 10 min** (5 × 25 min average; range 20-30 min each)
- **Grouping principle:** **one episode per content block (4) + one cross-block exam-rehearsal episode (1)**. The four content blocks (S2-S3, S4-S7, S8-S12, S13-S17) are the natural seams already drawn by the course; they map cleanly onto the cheat sheet and onto the way the mock exam mixes frameworks. The fifth episode does what no single-block episode can: it drills the **five mock-anchored frameworks** (EU AI Act, RACI, Kotter & Schlesinger resistance, Hahn et al., Make/Buy/Partner) head-to-head, walks the actual mock exam end-to-end, and rehearses the **cross-block patterns** (centralized↔federated tension across S4/S5/S17; speed-vs-rigor across the whole course; human-oversight spine).

**Why not fewer?**

- 3 episodes would force two blocks together (e.g. Blocks 2+3) and lose theme coherence — sourcing/portfolio has nothing in common with motivation theory beyond being in the same course.
- 4 episodes is tempting (1 per block) but leaves the mock exam un-drilled. The mock is the northern light; it deserves its own episode.

**Why not more?**

- 6+ episodes would split a block (e.g. S10/S11 People Analytics into its own episode) which fragments the people-leadership arc and adds listening time without new content.
- The student asked for minimum. 5 is the floor that still respects both the block structure and the exam.

**S1, S18, S19, S20 are out of scope.** S1 is intro/pre-course quiz (no exam content). S18 is team presentations. S19-S20 are wrap-up with no slides yet. The cheat sheet itself caps at S17. All exam-relevant material is covered.

## 2. Episode grouping

### Episode 1 · Sustainable AI — tensions, ethics, and the leader's lens

- **Source sessions:** S2, S3
- **Estimated length:** 25 min
- **Spine (in order):**
  1. The integrative sustainability frame — **Hahn et al. (2014)**: 3 dimensions × 3 levels × time × space, why "neutral" is a misrepresentation
  2. AI is a planetary supply chain — **Crawford (2021)** + **Gambacorta & Shreeti (2025)** ecosystem map
  3. The four families of AI sustainability challenges (environmental / economic-geopolitical / labor-inequality / power-rights) and the five families of barriers
  4. The role of the sustainable leader — reduce tensions, decrease trade-offs, "exceed narrowly, meet broadly"
  5. The EU AI Act risk tiers — unacceptable / high / limited / minimal, and the recruitment-HR trap
  6. The other three ethical-AI families — OECD principles, **Floridi & Cowls (2019)** five-principle synthesis, governance & lifecycle mechanisms
  7. Four leadership theories for ethical AI — transformational (Bass & Riggio), servant (Dierendonck & Nuijten), ethical (Brown et al.), moral courage (Edmondson; Hannah & Avolio); and why **Czakert & Wietrak (2025)** say we need a new model
  8. Building AI ethics as an *organizational capability* — **Wade & Tomoko (2024)**, **Blackman (2022)**
- **Key frameworks to anchor on:** Hahn et al. integrative sustainability; Crawford AI ecosystem; sustainability challenges/barriers; role of sustainable leaders; EU AI Act risk tiers; OECD AI Principles; Floridi & Cowls 5 principles; four ethics framework families; transformational / servant / ethical leadership; moral courage
- **Cases to weave in:** AI in healthcare (false positives), NYT vs OpenAI, facial recognition (denied entry to show)
- **Mock-exam connections:** **Q1.1 (EU AI Act, hiring AI = high-risk, human-in-the-loop trap)** and **Q2.1 (Hahn et al. applied to biased hiring AI — 3 levels + temporal + spatial)**. This episode owns two of the five mock-anchored frameworks.

## 3. The AI podcast prompt (ready to paste — tool-agnostic)

```
ROLE
You are producing a two-host conversational podcast for an MBA-level student preparing for the "AI for Sustainable Leadership" final exam (ESADE, course C8, 2025-2026). Hosts are: HOST A (the "framework anchor" — clear, structured, names every author and year), and HOST B (the "exam coach" — turns concepts into mock-exam-style triggers and walks through reasoning). Tone: warm, conversational, NOT lecture-style. Energy of two smart colleagues quizzing each other on a long walk.

EXAM FORMAT YOU ARE PREPARING THE STUDENT FOR
- 20 short MCQs (1 pt each, 20 pts) — single concept per question, "what is X" or "which approach fits this short scenario"
- 5 scenario MCQs (3 pts each, 15 pts) — a paragraph-long case, pick the answer that correctly applies a framework
- 3 open-ended essays (5 pts each, 15 pts) — recommend a course of action, justify with named frameworks
- Total: 50 pts in 2 hours, closed-book
The mock exam confirms these recurring test triggers: EU AI Act risk tiers, RACI, Kotter & Schlesinger resistance to change, Hahn et al. (2014) integrative sustainability, Make/Buy/Partner sourcing.

EPISODE STRUCTURE (~25 minutes; absolute max 30)
1. INTRO HOOK (~30 sec) — Host A opens with one real situation a leader would face this week that this episode helps with. No abstract framing. Land it in seconds.
2. FRAMEWORK DEEP-DIVE (~10 min) — Hosts trade examples for each framework on the spine. For EVERY framework you mention, say the name + author + year + one-line argument it gives the student ("Hahn et al. 2014 say sustainability is the simultaneous management of tensions across three dimensions, three levels, time, and space"). Then give one concrete situation.
3. MOCK-EXAM-STYLE MINI-MCQ RECAP (~5 min) — Host B reads a short scenario (3-4 sentences) and four MCQ options. Host A picks one, then walks through WHY each distractor is wrong and the right one is right. Do 2-3 of these per episode.
4. CASE-STUDY WEAVE (~5 min) — Pick one real course case (e.g. Air Canada chatbot, ManCo, NordSteel, RippleHire, Unity Health, Musk vs OpenAI, Helix Operations). Apply 2-3 frameworks from the episode to it in conversation.
5. CLOSE (~1 min) — Host A lists 3 takeaways (one sentence each) and names what to listen for next.

DRILLING RULES (apply EVERY episode regardless of topic)
The five MOCK-ANCHORED frameworks must be REINFORCED at least once per episode (a single sentence reference is enough; full deep-dive in their home episode):
1. EU AI Act risk tiers
2. RACI (R≠A; A is exactly one person)
3. Kotter & Schlesinger 6 approaches to resistance
4. Hahn et al. (2014) integrative sustainability
5. Make / Buy / Partner sourcing
When you cite one of these, explicitly say: "this is a mock-exam-tested framework."

CITATION RULE
Every time you name a framework, also say the author surname(s) and year. Example: "Stouten et al. 2018", "Edmondson 1999 for safety, 2020 for moral courage", "Bakker and Demerouti 2007". If the year is not in the source material, say "year not specified in the course materials" — never guess. NEVER invent or paraphrase an author who doesn't appear in the source cheat sheet.

FLAGGING RULE
When you say something is on the mock exam, say "MOCK-EXAM TESTED" out loud so the student's ear catches it.

FORBIDDEN
- Do NOT hallucinate authors, years, or frameworks. If unsure, drop it.
- Do NOT add generic AI/leadership content that is NOT in the source material (cheat sheet + session decks). This is exam-prep, not a podcast about AI in general.
- Do NOT exceed 30 minutes. If the spine is too long, compress, do not extend.
- Do NOT use jargon without immediately unpacking it in one sentence.
- Do NOT have one host monologue for more than 90 seconds. Bounce.

SOURCE MATERIAL
Use ONLY the cheat sheet and session decks I will paste. If you cannot ground a claim in those sources, leave it out.
```
